“The gender gap in education affects the long-term consequences for individuals and society. This gap exists due to cultural norms, parental influence, socioeconomic factors and limited access to resources, hindering girls' and women's ability to fully participate in education.”

WHY?

Girls face inequalities from birth that can persist throughout their lives, including in education. Access to education and educational opportunities are critical for equipping individuals with the skills and knowledge necessary to thrive in the modern world. However, gender disparities in education persist for various reasons, preventing girls from accessing the same opportunities as boys.

Therefore, addressing the gap in education equality between genders is crucial in order to promoting equality, ensuring that all individuals have the opportunity to reach their full potential.
